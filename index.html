I don't just use AI —
I build with it

Fourteen years of database engineering taught me how systems behave under real load. I'm now applying that same rigour to AI — building agents that solve my own problems, and bringing AI-native workflows into how I engineer, architect, and operate data platforms.

001
Health Agent● Personal Agent · Production
A personal health agent that reasons over years of my own medical history and gives real-time dietary guidance — send it a photo of a meal, get an instant call on whether it fits my profile. Deliberately self-hosted on my own infrastructure, keeping sensitive data under my own control rather than handing it to a third party.
002
Job-Search Agent● Autonomous · Running
An autonomous agent running on my own VPS that continuously scouts roles matching my profile across markets and sources, scores them against my criteria, and surfaces only the strong matches — eliminating the manual grind of checking a dozen job boards every day.
003
Vector Search at Scale● Production Architecture
Shipped MongoDB Atlas Vector Search to production at Contentstack for an AI-driven content generation feature — evaluated Pinecone and Milvus, chose Atlas for retrieval performance and data co-location. Real RAG architecture serving real users, not a demo.
How I Build

I work adversarially across models — figuring something out with one agent, then stress-testing the hypothesis against the others. I pit Claude, Codex, and Gemini against each other until the sharpest solution wins.

AI is a high-leverage tool, not a replacement for judgement — the architecture decisions still have to be right.
