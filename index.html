The Person

Built on depth, not just breadth

I have spent 14 years at the intersection where databases meet business outcomes. From classified government networks in Abu Dhabi to FinTech platforms processing millions of transactions, from 1,000-node Cassandra clusters at Visa to 65-cluster SaaS migrations at Contentstack — I operate in environments where database failures are not an option.

My approach is simple: understand the business problem first, then select the right technology. I have worked across MongoDB, Cassandra, Aurora PostgreSQL, MySQL, ClickHouse, Redis, Neo4j, DynamoDB, Oracle, and SQL Server. Each has its place. The skill is knowing which place.

Equally effective as a hands-on platform engineer building automation pipelines, a solutions architect designing multi-database architectures for AI workloads, a pre-sales technical advisor running proof-of-value engagements, and an engineering leader building teams from scratch.

Currently building AI-assisted database operations tools — personal agents, vector search architectures, and agentic infrastructure provisioning. The future of database operations is not manual.
